The 米/分 Formula: How Surface 切削速度 Is 计算d

Surface speed — measured in 米/分 (Surface Feet per Minute, imperial) or Vc (meters per minute, metric) — is the linear velocity at which the cutting edge passes through the material. It is the single most important parameter in machining because it directly determines cutting temperature, tool wear rate, and surface integrity.

Imperial (米/分): 米/分 = (π × D × 转/分) ÷ 12
Metric (Vc): Vc (m/min) = (π × D × 转/分) ÷ 1000

Where D = diameter in inches (imperial) or mm (metric), and 转速 = spindle revolutions per minute. This calculator works in all three modes — enter any two values, and the third is computed automatically.

Why Chip Load Affects Optimal Surface 切削速度

The relationship between 米/分 and chip load is often misunderstood. At higher surface speeds, the chip load per tooth must be maintained within the recommended range — otherwise the tool rubs instead of shearing, generating excessive heat regardless of 米/分. This is why a proper speeds-and-feeds calculation requires both surface speed AND chip load optimization. Use the Chip Load 计算器 after setting your 米/分 to verify the feed per tooth is adequate.

米/分 vs. 转/分: When to Use Each

转速 is a machine parameter — it's what you set on the spindle control. 米/分 is a process parameter — it's what actually determines cutting performance. The same 米/分 can require very different 转速 depending on diameter:

  • Small diameter (6mm end mill): 800 米/分 → ~12,000 转速
  • Large diameter (100mm face mill): 800 米/分 → ~780 转速

This is why tool libraries in CAM systems store 米/分, not 转速 — the CAM software calculates 转速 based on the programmed tool diameter. When setup changes require a different tool holder or extension, the 米/分 stays the same; only the 转速 changes.

Signs Your Surface 切削速度 Is Wrong

Too fast: Rapid flank wear, plastic deformation of the cutting edge, built-up edge, chatter, discolored chips (blue/purple), poor surface finish.
Too slow: BUE formation, high cutting forces, poor chip control, chatter, smearing on the workpiece surface, reduced productivity.

常见问题解答

What is 米/分 in machining? 米/分 (Surface Feet per Minute) is the linear speed at which a cutting tool edge travels through the workpiece material. It determines cutting temperature, tool life, and surface finish.

How do I calculate 米/分 from 转/分? Multiply π by the diameter in inches and 转/分, then divide by 12: 米/分 = (π × D × 转/分) ÷ 12. Use this calculator for instant results.

What 米/分 should I use for carbide end mills in stainless steel? For stainless 304, use 250-400 米/分 for roughing, 300-500 米/分 for finishing. Lower end for heavier cuts, higher end for finishing passes.

The difference between 米/分 and 转/分? 米/分 is cutting speed through material (process parameter). 转速 is spindle rotation speed (machine parameter). 转速 = (米/分 × 12) ÷ (π × D).

Does workpiece hardness affect surface speed? Yes — harder materials require lower 米/分. As a rule of thumb, reduce 米/分 by 20% for every 10 HRC increase above 35 HRC. Higher hardness also increases cutting forces, which must be accounted for in the speed and feed calculation.

Should I use coolant or dry at high 米/分? At high 米/分 (above 600 米/分 in steel), coolant is essential to manage thermal shock. With carbide tools, intermittent coolant application can cause micro-cracking. Use consistent flood or through-spindle coolant when running above 500 米/分.
