Automotive Machining

High-volume production with relentless cycle-time pressure. You need tools that hold tolerance across thousands of parts — and pricing that doesn't eat your margin.

Common materials

Gray Cast Iron (GCI)Ductile Iron4140 / 4340 Alloy SteelAISI 1045 Carbon SteelAlSi Aluminum AlloysSintered Powder Metal

Automotive machining splits into two worlds: ferrous (brake discs, crankshafts, camshafts, gears) and aluminum (engine blocks, transmission housings, suspension knuckles). The tooling strategy for each is completely different — CBN for high-volume iron, PCD for high-volume aluminum, and tough coated carbide for the steel forgings in between.

Tools we recommend

Turning InsertsCNMG, WNMG for shafts and discs. CBN for brake rotors. Wiper geometry for surface finish. Carbide End MillsIndexable face mills for deck faces. Solid carbide for oil galleries and precision bores. Carbide DrillsThrough-coolant for deep oil passages. 5×D–12×D for crankshaft oil holes. PCD & CBN InsertsCBN for high-volume cast iron brake rotors. PCD for aluminum engine components.
