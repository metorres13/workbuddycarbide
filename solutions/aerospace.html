Aerospace Machining

Titanium that eats carbide for breakfast. Inconel that work-hardens if you look at it wrong. Aerospace materials demand the right grade, the right coating, and the right parameters — or you lose a $50,000 forging.

Common materials

Ti-6Al-4VInconel 718WaspaloyAluminum 7075-T6Al-Li Alloys17-4PH StainlessA286

Aerospace is the ultimate test of your tooling strategy. Titanium conducts heat poorly — you're cutting with the tool tip, not the chip. Inconel work-hardens instantly if your feed is too low. And aluminum-airframe parts have 95% material removal rates with wall thicknesses under 1 mm. Each material is its own puzzle.

Tools we recommend

Carbide End MillsTiSiN or AlCrN coating for titanium. Corner-radius for roughing, ball-nose for 5-axis finishing of structural ribs. Turning InsertsS-grade carbide for superalloy turning. Positive rake, sharp edge prep — no honing for titanium finishing. Carbide DrillsThrough-coolant mandatory for superalloys. 5×D for standard holes, gun drills for deep cooling passages. Thread MillsThread milling over tapping for titanium and Inconel — one broken tap and your part is scrap.
