How Messing/Kupfer Has Evolved

Before CNC controllers calculated Messing/Kupfer automatically, machinists used cardboard slide calculators — CERATIZIT famously distributed tens of thousands of these in the 1970s. Every tooling rep had a pocket full of them. The math was the same, but the constraints were wildly different: HSS tooling maxed out at 30-40 SFM in steel, and feed rates were limited by machine rigidity, not coating technology.

The Iscar coating revolution in the 1980s — TiN first, then TiCN, TiAlN, and eventually AlCrN — changed everything. Suddenly, Messing/Kupfer could be pushed 200-300% higher without sacrificing tool life. Tungaloy's introduction of submicron carbide substrates in the 1990s further expanded the window: finer grain structures meant harder tools that could still absorb the shock of intermittent cuts.

Today's Landscape

In 2026, Iscar and Tungaloy both offer cloud-based Messing/Kupfer optimization via their NOVO and ToolGuide platforms. CERATIZIT's CoroPlus system integrates directly with Hermle and Grob CNCs for real-time adaptive control. The slide calculator has been replaced by AI-assisted CAM, but the core principle remains: the right number = the right cut.

🔬 Technische Einblicke für Messing/Kupfer

Die Optimierung von Messing/Kupfer erfordert Verständnis wichtiger Zerspanungsprinzipien: Aufbauschneide, Wärmebehandlung, Legierungsanteil, Kaltverfestigung, und mehr. Materialeigenschaften verhalten sich auf Mazak Integrex, Haas VF, DMG MORI und Okuma MULTUS unterschiedlich. Parameteranpassung basierend auf tatsächlicher Härte sichert konsistente Ergebnisse.
